Ages 13-17


Summary: Teens, while not nearly as affected as adults, don't do as well as the younger groups. Perhaps from interacting with other people more at school or in hangouts, their case and hospitalization rates both increase significantly from younger age groups. Due to teenagers' generally strong immune system, however, there was only one recorded death.


From the graph, we see that teens too have lower numbers in all categories compared to the average with 46% less cases than the average, 91.6% less hospitalizations and only one death per 100,000."
